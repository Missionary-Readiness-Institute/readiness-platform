# Branding Guide

This starter guide defines the current branding direction for Missionary Readiness Institute and the Missionary Readiness Platform. It is intended to support writing, design, MkDocs site updates, and future Codex work.

## 1. Current Branding Direction

The current direction is strong in several ways:

- **Clear purpose:** The language consistently centers missionary readiness rather than vague inspiration.
- **Credible restraint:** The site avoids claiming accreditation, institutional authority, or sending authority.
- **Useful audience focus:** Independent missionaries, churches, sponsors, families, and small organizations are named clearly.
- **Appropriate posture:** The tone emphasizes humility, accountability, safety, cross-cultural respect, and service.

Areas to keep refining:

- **Name clarity:** Decide whether the public brand should emphasize "Institute," "Platform," or both.
- **Tagline consistency:** Use one primary tagline across the site once chosen.
- **Visual identity:** Keep the design mature and warm without looking corporate, academic-heavy, or overly casual.
- **Public positioning:** Continue presenting the project as early-stage but serious, not as a finished training ecosystem.

## 2. Name Options

### Missionary Readiness Institute

**Pros:** Mature, memorable, and suitable for a serious training resource. Signals organized learning without sounding like a single course.

**Cons:** "Institute" may imply more formal structure than currently exists. Pair it with clear language that the project is not an accredited school, seminary, mission board, or sending agency.

### Missionary Readiness Platform

**Pros:** Accurate for the current GitHub Pages / MkDocs training site. Sounds practical, flexible, and still developing.

**Cons:** Less personal and less memorable than "Institute." May sound technical rather than ministry-oriented.

### Missionary Readiness Institute Platform

**Pros:** Connects the organization-style name with the practical site.

**Cons:** Slightly long. Best used in explanatory copy, not as the primary public name.

### Readiness for Mission

**Pros:** Plain, warm, and less institutional.

**Cons:** Less distinctive. Could be confused with a generic phrase or course title.

### Recommended Use

Use **Missionary Readiness Institute** as the primary public name. Use **Missionary Readiness Platform** to describe the training site itself.

Example:

> Missionary Readiness Institute is developing the Missionary Readiness Platform, a practical training site for missionary preparation.

## 3. Tagline Options

Recommended primary tagline:

> Practical training for faithful cross-cultural service.

Other strong options:

- Preparing workers for faithful cross-cultural service.
- Practical readiness for responsible mission work.
- Helping missionaries, churches, and sponsors prepare wisely.
- Training for humble, accountable, cross-cultural service.
- Readiness resources for thoughtful mission preparation.

Avoid taglines that promise outcomes or imply accreditation, such as "certifying missionaries for the nations" or "the complete missionary training solution."

## 4. Tone And Writing Style

The writing should feel:

- Professional but not corporate.
- Sober but not cold.
- Practical but not merely technical.
- Faithful but not triumphalistic.
- Mission-focused but not militarized.
- Humble about the platform's current stage.

Style guidance:

- Use plain Markdown headings and short sections.
- Prefer concrete nouns and direct verbs.
- Explain the problem without dramatizing it.
- Use "emerging," "developing," "starter," and "introductory" where accurate.
- Name boundaries clearly: not accredited, not a degree-granting institution, not a sending agency, not professional advice.
- Write for thoughtful Adventist and broader Christian readers without unnecessary denominational jargon.

## 5. Visual Direction

### Typography Feel

Use readable, restrained typography. The site should feel like a serious training resource, not a marketing brochure.

Recommended feel:

- Clear sans-serif body text.
- Calm, readable headings.
- Generous spacing for course material and policy pages.
- Avoid decorative, handwritten, distressed, or overly academic type.

For MkDocs Material, the default typography is acceptable unless a future design pass intentionally changes it.

### Color Direction

Current MkDocs Material colors use indigo and blue. This is a workable starter direction: calm, trustworthy, and familiar.

Recommended palette feel:

- Deep blue or indigo for trust and stability.
- Muted green or earth accent for growth, service, and groundedness.
- Warm neutral backgrounds for readability.
- High-contrast dark mode support.

Avoid:

- Aggressive red/black combinations.
- Overly bright neon colors.
- Heavy gold/purple prestige styling that implies formal academic authority.
- Visual palettes that feel militarized, nationalistic, or triumphalist.

### Logo Concepts

Early logo concepts should be simple and restrained:

- A path or waymark suggesting preparation and next steps.
- An open book or field guide suggesting learning.
- A simple compass mark suggesting orientation, not conquest.
- A modest wordmark using the full name.

Avoid:

- Weapons, shields, battle imagery, or conquest symbols.
- Colonial map imagery or dominant global arrows.
- Overly ornate seals that imply accreditation or formal academic authority.
- Symbols that suggest endorsement, commissioning, or institutional control.

### Imagery Guidance

Use imagery sparingly and respectfully.

Prefer:

- Realistic training, discussion, mentoring, reading, travel preparation, or community-learning imagery.
- Diverse people shown with dignity and consent.
- Images that emphasize listening, learning, service, and preparation.

Avoid:

- Poverty-as-backdrop images.
- Savior-complex visuals.
- Children used as emotional fundraising props.
- Exoticized culture imagery.
- Militarized or conquest-oriented mission imagery.

## 6. Language To Use And Avoid

### Use

- Missionary readiness
- Faithful service
- Cross-cultural humility
- Practical preparation
- Local churches and local leaders
- Accountability
- Safety and care
- Responsible support
- Certificate of completion
- Emerging platform
- Introductory training material
- Thoughtful preparation

### Avoid

- Accredited certification, unless accreditation is actually obtained
- Degree, seminary, university, or institute language that implies formal academic authority
- Deploy, conquer, take territory, mobilize an army, or other militarized ministry language
- Save the nations, rescue the unreached, or savior-complex framing
- Complete solution, guaranteed success, fully mature ecosystem, or similar overclaims
- Language that bypasses local churches, local leaders, or family safety
- Fundraising pressure, guilt-based appeals, or emotional manipulation

## 7. Brand Positioning Statement

Missionary Readiness Institute is an emerging Christian training initiative developing practical readiness resources for independent missionaries, church members, mission-minded families, local churches, sponsors, and small organizations. The platform helps learners prepare for faithful cross-cultural service through biblical faithfulness, humility, accountability, safety, and practical planning, without claiming accreditation, sending authority, or professional advisory status.

## 8. Compact Brand Rules

Use this compact version in `AGENTS.md`, project notes, or future design briefs:

- Primary name: **Missionary Readiness Institute**.
- Platform descriptor: **Missionary Readiness Platform**.
- Preferred tagline: **Practical training for faithful cross-cultural service.**
- Tone: professional, sober, practical, humble, mission-oriented, and service-focused.
- Audience: independent missionaries, church members, mission-minded families, local churches, sponsors, and small organizations.
- Positioning: an emerging Christian training platform, not an accredited school, seminary, mission board, sending agency, or degree-granting institution.
- Visual feel: mature, readable, calm, trustworthy, and simple enough for MkDocs Material.
- Use: readiness, biblical faithfulness, cross-cultural humility, accountability, safety, local churches, local leaders, responsible support, certificate of completion.
- Avoid: hype, manipulation, savior-complex language, militarized ministry language, colonial framing, exaggerated promises, and accreditation claims.
