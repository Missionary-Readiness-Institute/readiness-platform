
# Lesson 03: The 5-Tier Evacuation Protocol

## Key Idea

Clear departure guidance from low threat to critical emergency.

## Lesson Goal

Learn how this principle supports faithful fieldwork, local ownership, and long-term effectiveness.

## What This Lesson Teaches

- Clear departure guidance from low threat to critical emergency.
- Why a shallow or foreign method can undermine local multiplication.
- How to choose one practical habit to apply immediately.

## The 5-Tier Evacuation Trigger Matrix

| Threat Level | Description | Action Triggers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Level 1: Little to No Threat** | Stable environment. | Register with the embassy and develop a Standard Operating Security Plan (SOSP). |
| **Level 2: Low Threat (Precautionary Alert)** | Early socio-political deterioration. | Fully fuel vehicles, verify Go-Bags are packed, designate central meeting places, and verify emergency cash reserves. |
| **Level 3: Medium Threat (Authorized Departure)** | Events posing actual threats have occurred. | Field Director may authorize non-essential staff and dependents to leave for a safe neighboring country. |
| **Level 4: High Threat (Ordered Evacuation)** | Unabated deterioration. | All international personnel are ordered to evacuate immediately; operations suspended or handed to national staff. |
| **Level 5: Critical (Catastrophic)** | Sudden, catastrophic event where contact with HQ is impossible. | On-the-ground Team Leader has full Decision-Making Authority (DMA) to act immediately to save lives and move to the nearest safe location. |

Identify one current approach in your context that should be changed to support sustainable local work.
