# Team Dynamics, Leadership, and Conflict Repair

Team Dynamics, Leadership, and Conflict Repair equips teams to manage high-stakes interpersonal issues, prevent burnout, and restore trust using proven frameworks.

!!! note "Level 2 course"
    This course is designed for team leaders, field practitioners, and support partners who want healthier team culture.

## Course Purpose

To help teams build resilient, decentralized leadership; navigate difficult conversations; repair conflict; and share burdens in healthy ways.

## Intended Audience

- Small ministry teams and local leadership groups.
- Sending partners and mentors.
- Field leaders responsible for team health and mission unity.

## Prerequisites

Recommended: Spiritual Readiness and Resilience and Cross-Cultural Service.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners should be able to:

- Describe decentralized, starfish-style leadership.
- Use *Crucial Conversations* principles to handle high-stakes dialogue.
- Recognize self-deception and shift to people-focused thinking.
- Build team rhythms for shared burden and debriefing.

## Estimated Time

60-75 minutes.

## Practical Output

A Team Covenant and a Conflict Repair Plan.

## Suggested Reading & Resources

- *Crucial Conversations* — high-stakes team dialogue.
- *Leadership and Self-Deception* — moving from self-centered to people-centered thinking.
- *The Anatomy of Peace* — conflict repair and heart change.
- *The Starfish and the Spider* — resilient, decentralized leadership.

## Reflection Questions

1. How can your team move away from top-down control?
2. What hard conversation do you need to have first?
3. Who will help carry the next season of work and care?

## Completion Criteria

- Review all lessons.
- Draft a Team Covenant and Conflict Repair Plan.
- Share them with a teammate or mentor.
