# Field Resilience, Team Health, and Reentry

Field Resilience, Team Health, and Reentry is a Level 2 Field Practitioner course for workers who need practical tools for psychological care, multicultural team health, and transition resilience in cross-cultural service.

!!! note "Field Practitioner course"
    This course is designed for active workers, tentmakers, and field teams who need resilient practices for person formation, team culture, and reentry.

## Course purpose

This course teaches field practitioners how to build personal resilience, manage cultural distance, adapt to team subculture, care for single workers and families, and use the RAFT principle for healthy reentry.

## Intended audience

- Active cross-cultural workers and teams
- Support leaders responsible for worker care
- Individuals preparing for long-term service or furlough

## Prerequisites

Recommended: [Missionary Readiness 101](../missionary-readiness-101/index.md), [Field Readiness](../field-readiness/index.md), or [Missionary Care](../missionary-care/index.md).

## Learning objectives

By the end of this course, learners should be able to:

- Describe why person formation is as important as missiological training
- Assess cultural distance using the E-Scale
- Adapt to both host culture and team culture
- Support singles, families, and TCKs in diverse field settings
- Use RAFT to plan healthy return or furlough transitions

## Estimated time

90-110 minutes.

## Practical output

A resilience plan, E-Scale assessment, team covenant template, RAFT transition checklist, and a reentry debrief guide.

## Required reading/viewing

- Read this course overview.
- Complete all five lessons and at least one tool.
- Apply one resilience strategy in a real team or transition situation.

## Reflection questions

1. When has cross-cultural stress revealed an old wound or family issue for you?
2. Which E-Scale level fits your current calling?
3. What does a healthy farewell look like in your context?

## Practical assignment

Complete the E-Scale Ministry Assessment and the RAFT Transition Checklist for your next field assignment or furlough.

## Completion criteria

- Review all lessons and one tool page.
- Use the E-Scale and RAFT templates in a real planning conversation.
- Share your resilience plan with a mentor or support team.

## Facilitator notes

- Emphasize the importance of person formation and psychological care.
- Encourage team agreements before field deployment.
- Review reentry plans with experienced local and sending leaders.
