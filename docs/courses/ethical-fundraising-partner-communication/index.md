# Ethical Fundraising & Partner Communication

Ethical Fundraising & Partner Communication equips field practitioners to build support-raising systems that honor donors, protect local dignity, and treat money as a means to mission, not an end.

!!! note "Level 2 course"
    This course is designed for independent workers, support teams, and field communicators who need ethical financial practices in challenging contexts.

## Course Purpose

To help learners overcome the shame of asking for support, invite partners into mission responsibly, and manage gifts with integrity.

## Intended Audience

- Independent missionaries and project leaders.
- Support-raising coordinators.
- Church and team communication partners.

## Prerequisites

Recommended: [Missionary Readiness 101](../missionary-readiness-101/index.md) and [Field Readiness](../field-readiness/index.md).

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners should be able to:

- Explain the biblical foundation for support raising.
- Build a partnership team without reducing people to transactions.
- Manage restricted and unrestricted gifts with clear policies.
- Tell ethical stories that protect local neighbors.
- Communicate safely in sensitive and restricted contexts.

## Estimated Time

70-90 minutes.

## Practical Output

A risk-screened communication checklist, a media consent form, and a gift acceptance policy.

- *Funding Your Ministry* — principles for ethical support raising.
- *The God Ask* — inviting partners into a shared calling.
- *People Raising* — relationships and support-team development.

## Reflection Questions

1. How will you frame support raising as invitation rather than obligation?
2. What risks should you screen for in donor communications?
3. Who should review your gift acceptance policy before it is used?

## Completion Criteria

- Review all five lessons.
- Complete the risk guide, consent form, and gift acceptance policy.
- Share your planned communication process with a mentor or sending leader.
