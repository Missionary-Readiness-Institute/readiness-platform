# Lesson 03: The Crisis Management Team (CMT) & Communications

## Lesson overview

The Crisis Management Team (CMT) is the nerve center for a severe incident. It protects the field from panic and manages official information.

## Key idea

A CMT insulates crisis work from routine operations, keeps the field focused, and controls communication flow.

## Why this matters

When field workers are left without a crisis team, they can make rushed or unsafe decisions under stress.

## Field context

The CMT is activated in major incidents such as kidnapping, violent unrest, political collapse, or sudden mass displacement.

## Leader role

Leaders should appoint a CMT, define roles clearly, and establish communication protocols before activation.

## Team role

Team members must trust the CMT, follow instructions, and avoid sharing unauthorized information.

## Preparation

- Define the CMT membership and roles.
- Create a communication flow chart.
- Identify spokespeople and information gates.

## CMT roles explained

The CMT typically includes a crisis manager, communications lead, legal advisor, member care lead, and logistics coordinator.

## Step 1: Activate the CMT

When the trigger threshold is crossed, the Crisis Manager activates the team and announces the crisis protocol.

## Step 2: Manage communications

The CMT controls official news releases, internal updates, and contact with family/supporters.

## Step 3: Advise and support the field

The CMT provides guidance to the field without exposing them to unnecessary pressure or conflicting instructions.

## Common challenges

Field teams may receive multiple messages from different sources. The CMT must make one official line and stop rumor spread.

## Practical example

In a high-risk incident, the CMT authorizes one spokesperson, sets a holding statement, and informs the field of the next step.

## Reflection questions

1. Who should be on your CMT?
2. How would your CMT handle press, family, and internal communications?
3. What would stop the field from receiving conflicting directives?

## Summary

A strong CMT keeps crises contained and the field supported. Communication discipline is a core part of that strength.

## Next step

Draft a simple CMT charter that defines roles, authority, and communication flow.
