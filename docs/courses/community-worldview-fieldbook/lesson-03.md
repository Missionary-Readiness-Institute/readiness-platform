# Lesson 3: The Illustration Filter (Communicating in Context)

## Course/series
Community & Worldview Fieldbook

## Audience
- Field workers preparing stories, sermons, and teaching illustrations
- Teams wanting to communicate clearly across cultural boundaries
- Leaders who need a practical filter for Gospel illustration

## Purpose
Evaluate teaching illustrations through five categories and prioritize stories that local people understand and can connect to the Gospel.

## Learning objectives
- Describe the five categories of illustration relevance
- Distinguish Category 1 from Category 4 illustrations
- Use Category 5 as a bridge for Gospel communication

## Core principle
Effective Gospel communication uses stories that the local community already understands, not foreign concepts they cannot receive.

## Field problem
Teachers often use illustrations that make sense only in a foreign culture, leaving locals confused or disconnected from the Gospel message.

## Key concepts
- Category 1: foreign concepts nobody understands
- Category 4: locally experienced and understood stories
- Category 5: experienced but not understood, useful as a Gospel bridge

## Practical framework
Use the Illustration Filter Matrix to place each story in a category and choose the highest category that still aligns with the Gospel.

## Scenario or case exercise
A preacher uses a Western parable about bankers and contracts in a community where those institutions are unfamiliar, creating a Category 1 illustration.

## Checklist or worksheet
- List the story’s images and assumptions
- Assess whether the community experiences them firsthand
- If not, ask whether a Category 4 or 5 story exists instead

## Discussion questions
1. What story categories are common in your current teaching?
2. Which local experience could become a better Gospel illustration?
3. How can Category 5 help explain spiritual mystery without introducing foreign theology?

## Field assignment
Build an illustration filter for one teaching point. Choose a Category 4 example if available, or a Category 5 bridge that leads naturally to the Gospel.
